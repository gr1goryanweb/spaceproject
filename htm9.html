Black Hole

A region where gravity is so intense that the escape velocity exceeds the speed of light, c. Spacetime curvature becomes infinite at the center - not even photons can escape.

The Sun

Solar System

Our star system consists of a G2V yellow dwarf and the objects that orbit it, bound by Newton's law of universal gravitation. Eight planets trace elliptical paths through spacetime.

Planet I

Mercury

The smallest planet. Because it lacks an atmosphere, temperatures fluctuate from 430°C to -180°C. Its heavily cratered surface bears the scars of a violent solar system youth.

Planet II

Venus

The hottest planet. A runaway greenhouse effect traps solar energy, creating surface pressures 90 times that of Earth and temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Planet III

Earth

Our home. The only known world with liquid water and an atmosphere containing 21% oxygen, vital for life. A magnetic field shields us from cosmic radiation.

Planet IV

Mars

The Red Planet. Its rusted iron surface and thin atmosphere make it a prime candidate for future human colonization. Olympus Mons is the tallest volcano in the solar system.

Planet V

Jupiter

The King of Planets. This gas giant is so massive it could fit all other planets inside it twice over. The Great Red Spot is a storm larger than Earth that has raged for centuries.

Planet VI

Saturn

The ringed wonder. Its iconic rings are composed primarily of ice particles and rocky debris spanning 282,000 km. Saturn is the least dense planet - it could float on water.

Planet VII

Uranus

The tilted giant. Uranus rotates at an angle of 98 degrees, likely due to a massive ancient collision. Its methane atmosphere absorbs red light, giving it a pale aqua hue.

Planet VIII

Neptune

The windiest world. Supersonic winds reach 2,100 km/h through clouds of frozen methane on this deep blue ice giant, the most distant planet from our Sun.
